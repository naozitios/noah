# Mapletree Logistics Trust (SGX: M44U)

*May 4, 2025*

## Is an APAC Logistics REIT Really Recession-Proof?

Mapletree Logistics Trust (MLT) has long been viewed as one of Singapore's premier logistics REITs, benefiting from geographically diversified warehouse assets and stable rental income.

However, recent earnings suggest that operational strength alone may not be enough to offset broader macroeconomic headwinds.

Despite reporting **96.2% portfolio occupancy** and **5.1% positive rental reversions**, the stock declined approximately **7%** following its latest earnings release.

The market appeared to focus less on operating performance and more on several structural concerns:

- Lower revenue contribution from China
- Lost income from recently divested properties
- Foreign currency depreciation against the Singapore dollar

While the business itself remains fundamentally healthy, I believe investors are increasingly pricing in slower growth and a more challenging operating environment.

---

# Investment Thesis

> **Near-term:** Bearish  
> **Long-term:** Neutral to cautiously positive

MLT remains a high-quality logistics REIT with disciplined capital management and resilient occupancy.

However, I believe several factors limit its upside over the near term:

- Significant exposure to China
- Reduced contribution from divested assets
- Lower expected DPU growth
- Continued uncertainty surrounding global trade

Although I remain constructive on the business over the long run, I don't believe current conditions provide an attractive entry point.

---

# Strong Operations Don't Always Translate Into Strong Returns

Operationally, MLT continues to execute well.

Recent results highlighted:

- **96.2% occupancy**
- **5.1% positive rental reversions**
- Stable portfolio management
- Continued capital recycling

Ordinarily, these would be positive signals.

Instead, the market focused on weaker forward-looking drivers.

This serves as a reminder that markets price future expectations rather than past performance.

---

# Diversification Can Also Mean Exposure

One of MLT's biggest strengths is its geographic diversification across Asia-Pacific.

But diversification also introduces macroeconomic exposure.

China currently contributes approximately:

- **17% of gross revenue**
- **16.1% of net lettable area (NLA)**

With China's property market and broader economy remaining under pressure, weakness in leasing conditions could continue weighing on portfolio performance.

There are some encouraging signs.

Retail spending in China has begun showing modest improvement, suggesting consumer demand may gradually recover.

Whether that translates into stronger industrial and logistics demand remains uncertain.

For now, China remains more of a headwind than a catalyst.

---

# Capital Recycling Is Becoming More Conservative

Management has indicated that future divestments will prioritize:

- Higher-quality assets
- Long-term growth opportunities
- Portfolio resilience

From a strategic perspective, this is sensible.

However, it also creates a short-term trade-off.

Divesting mature assets and redeploying capital often results in a temporary reduction in distributable income until replacement assets begin contributing.

In other words:

- lower near-term DPU
- potentially stronger long-term portfolio quality

I view this as positive for the business but less attractive for income-focused investors over the next few quarters.

---

# Interest Rate and Currency Hedging

One area where MLT continues to perform well is risk management.

Currently:

- **75% of income** is currency hedged into SGD.
- **81% of debt** is fixed-rate.

These hedges significantly reduce earnings volatility.

With Singapore interest rates gradually declining, any remaining floating-rate debt could become cheaper to finance, partially offsetting currency appreciation.

That said, the portfolio's financing remains globally diversified.

As of March 31, 2024:

- only **3%** of interest payments were denominated in SGD
- roughly **11%** were denominated in JPY

Management estimates that every **25 basis point** movement in interest rates changes annual DPU by roughly **0.04 Singapore cents**, indicating relatively limited sensitivity.

---

# Tariffs Remain An Important Risk

Global supply chains continue evolving.

Large multinational companies such as Apple and Roche have already begun shifting portions of manufacturing outside China.

Although management estimates only around **15% of tenants** are directly involved in export-oriented businesses, broader supply chain adjustments could still affect logistics demand over time.

Trade uncertainty therefore remains an important variable.

MLT proved this during the post-"Liberation Day" sell-off, where the stock declined approximately **22.5%** as investors reassessed global trade expectations.

The trust is clearly not insulated from geopolitical developments.

- Tariff tensions ease
- China's economy begins recovering
- Rental reversions improve toward portfolio averages
- Divestment proceeds are successfully reinvested

# How I'm Positioning

Although MLT remains a fundamentally strong REIT, I believe near-term risks outweigh potential returns.

My view is therefore relatively straightforward:

- I **would not buy** the stock today.
- I **would not short** the stock either.
- I would simply wait.

While my quantitative model suggests additional downside over the coming week, the expected move is too small to justify initiating a short position.

If trade tensions ease or China's economy begins recovering more meaningfully, my outlook would likely become more constructive.

Until then, I believe there are more attractive opportunities elsewhere.

---

# Final Thoughts

Mapletree Logistics Trust continues to demonstrate disciplined management, high occupancy, and prudent balance sheet management.

The issue isn't operational quality.

It's macroeconomic exposure.

China remains an important contributor to earnings, global trade uncertainty continues to cloud demand, and slower DPU growth reduces the trust's appeal for income investors.

Over the long run, MLT is still a quality logistics REIT.

But quality businesses aren't always attractive investments at every point in the cycle.

For now, I believe patience is the better position.
