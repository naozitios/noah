# CoreWeave: Bullish Today, Bearish Tomorrow?

_September 26, 2025_

The **December 2025 $135 price target** for CoreWeave rests on solid fundamentals. Forecasting newly listed companies is inherently difficult, but the assumptions behind a **40× EV/EBIT multiple** and a **CY26 EBIT estimate of $2.57B** are reasonable.

CoreWeave has executed exceptionally well by filling a supply gap left by hyperscalers. However, I believe the market is underestimating several structural risks that could determine whether the company becomes a durable infrastructure leader or simply a beneficiary of today's GPU shortage.

This isn't intended to be a full investment report or valuation model. Instead, it's a concise overview of the questions I think matter most—and how they influence my own investment decisions.

---

# Investment Thesis

> **Short-term:** Bullish  
> **Long-term:** Cautiously bearish (unless the AI compute market expands significantly)

My current view is straightforward.

CoreWeave is likely to continue benefiting from:

- Strong enterprise AI demand
- Limited GPU supply
- Its positioning as a pure-play AI infrastructure company
- Investor enthusiasm surrounding AI infrastructure

Longer term, however, the investment depends on three questions:

1. Can CoreWeave build meaningful switching costs?
2. Will hyperscalers vertically integrate AI infrastructure?
3. Does AI become sufficiently commoditized to dramatically expand the total compute market?

Today, I think the market prices in the first outcome while underpricing the latter two.

---

# Competition Is Coming From Every Direction

## Hyperscalers Are Not Standing Still

Microsoft Azure, AWS and Google Cloud have no intention of giving up AI infrastructure.

Microsoft alone announced roughly **$80 billion of AI infrastructure investment**, signalling that hyperscalers intend to own as much of the AI stack as possible.

Rather than viewing CoreWeave as a permanent competitor, I see it more as a solution that exists because hyperscalers currently cannot satisfy demand quickly enough.

Interestingly, we also see very little strategic investment from hyperscalers into independent neocloud providers.

That suggests they expect to compete directly rather than partner with them.

Today, many AI developers choose providers like CoreWeave because they offer:

- Better GPU availability
- Lower costs
- More HPC-focused infrastructure
- Faster deployment

However, once Azure and other hyperscalers fully scale their GPU fleets, customer behaviour may change.

If a company already stores its data inside Azure, training and inference naturally become easier there than moving workloads elsewhere.

Convenience becomes a competitive advantage.

---

# CoreWeave's Competitive Position

Compared with other neocloud providers such as:

- Lambda
- Nebius
- Crusoe

CoreWeave clearly has the strongest market position today.

The question is whether that lead becomes a moat.

## Features Are Not Moats

CoreWeave's competitive advantages are often described as:

- Better customer success
- Security
- Storage
- Monitoring
- Higher GPU utilization (MFUs)
- Strong networking
- Slurm + Kubernetes integration
- Proprietary technologies such as CKS, SUNK and Tensorizer

These are certainly strengths.

But most are engineering advantages rather than structural advantages.

Customer success improves with investment.

Storage and monitoring can be replicated or purchased through SaaS vendors.

Networking performance improves as competitors spend more capital.

Even CoreWeave's distributed training optimizations ultimately rely on engineering talent—a resource that is valuable, but not scarce.

None of these appear impossible to reproduce over time.

---

# Switching Costs Matter More Than Features

History shows that early technology leaders remain dominant when customers become expensive to replace.

Cloud infrastructure traditionally enjoys relatively low switching costs.

Moving compute workloads is rarely painless, but it is significantly easier than replacing an ERP system or rebuilding an enterprise workflow platform.

CoreWeave benefits from some ecosystem effects:

- Engineers become familiar with the platform.
- Communities share operational knowledge.
- Companies hire experienced users.

These create useful tailwinds.

But they are not the same as deep switching costs.

If another provider offers similar performance at lower prices, customers can move.

That makes long-term defensibility difficult.

---

# Is the Neocloud Market Actually Sustainable?

This is the question I find most interesting.

## Today's Customers

Neocloud customers generally fall into three groups:

- AI startups
- Research institutions
- Large companies that cannot yet secure enough GPU capacity

These customers value immediate access more than absolute cost efficiency.

But I don't believe this remains true forever.

## What Happens As The Market Matures?

The largest AI companies increasingly have incentives to own their own infrastructure.

Dedicated infrastructure becomes cheaper at scale.

Large organizations also gain:

- Lower operating costs
- Better optimization
- More control
- Greater security
- Better compliance

OpenAI's reported **$100B infrastructure agreement** is an early signal of this trend.

As GPU shortages ease, I expect many large AI companies to transition away from renting compute and toward owning it.

If that happens, CoreWeave's customer mix shifts toward:

- Smaller startups
- Research labs
- Mid-sized AI companies

Those customers are valuable—but likely less profitable than today's hyperscale contracts.

---

# Is It Checkmate?

Not necessarily.

CoreWeave still has several paths toward building a durable business.

## First-Mover Advantage

Being the infrastructure provider behind OpenAI gives CoreWeave credibility that competitors cannot easily replicate.

Large customer wins often create additional enterprise demand.

Trust compounds.

## Knowledge Effects

As engineers move between companies, familiarity with CoreWeave spreads.

Communities produce tutorials, tooling and operational best practices.

These effects strengthen the ecosystem, even if they are weaker than traditional network effects.

## Lower Cost Of Capital

If CoreWeave can consistently raise capital more cheaply than competitors, it gains an important strategic advantage.

Infrastructure businesses are capital intensive.

Cheaper capital allows faster deployment and more aggressive expansion.

## AI Compute Could Become Commoditized

This is the scenario where I become significantly more bullish.

If model training and inference become commoditized, many more organizations may decide to build and operate their own AI models.

Instead of a few giant customers, the market becomes thousands.

That dramatically expands the total addressable market.

Security and compliance could reinforce this trend.

Many enterprises may prefer training models inside dedicated environments rather than relying on shared infrastructure.

That is exactly the niche where CoreWeave could thrive.

---

# How I'm Positioning

Today, I remain **short-term bullish**.

CoreWeave benefits from:

- AI enthusiasm
- Strong revenue growth
- Limited GPU supply
- Pure-play AI positioning

Those factors may continue pushing the stock beyond intrinsic value.

However, I expect volatility to increase as investors begin pricing:

- Contract concentration
- Switching costs
- Competitive pressure
- Hyperscaler expansion

My view changes depending on how AI infrastructure evolves.

### I Become More Bullish If

- AI training becomes commoditized.
- Inference demand explodes.
- Thousands of companies begin operating their own models.
- The GPU compute market expands dramatically.

### I Become More Bearish If

- Hyperscalers aggressively vertically integrate.
- OpenAI increasingly builds and owns infrastructure.
- GPU supply normalizes faster than demand grows.
- CoreWeave struggles to establish durable switching costs.

If those bearish scenarios materialize, I would likely rotate capital away from CoreWeave.

---

# Final Thoughts

CoreWeave has become one of the defining infrastructure companies of the current AI cycle.

The company has executed exceptionally well, but execution alone does not guarantee a durable competitive advantage.

Ultimately, I believe the investment comes down to a simple question:

> **Will AI infrastructure become more centralized or more democratized?**

If AI remains concentrated among a handful of hyperscalers and frontier labs, CoreWeave's long-term opportunity narrows considerably.

If AI compute becomes commoditized and thousands of companies build and deploy their own models, CoreWeave could become one of the major beneficiaries.

For now, I'm happy to ride the current momentum—but I'm watching the competitive landscape much more closely than the next earnings report.
