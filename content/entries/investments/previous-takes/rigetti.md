# Rigetti Computing (NASDAQ: RGTI)

_June 24, 2025_

## Betting on the Future of Quantum Computing

Quantum computing remains one of the most speculative technology investments available today.

Commercial adoption is still years away, revenue remains limited, and viable business applications are only beginning to emerge.

Yet history has shown that foundational computing platforms often create enormous value long before widespread commercialization.

Rigetti is one of the few publicly traded companies offering investors direct exposure to quantum hardware.

While the business remains early-stage, I believe the company is well positioned to benefit if quantum computing becomes commercially viable over the next decade.

# Why Quantum Computing Matters

Quantum computing represents an entirely different computational paradigm.

Unlike classical computers that process information using binary bits, quantum computers operate using **qubits**, allowing calculations through:

- Superposition
- Entanglement
- Quantum interference

These properties allow certain classes of problems to be solved exponentially faster than classical hardware.

Potential applications include:

- Drug discovery
- Molecular simulation
- Financial optimization
- Supply chain optimization
- Machine learning
- Cryptography

Much like GPUs became essential infrastructure for artificial intelligence, quantum processors could eventually become foundational infrastructure for solving computational problems beyond classical limits.

# Why Rigetti?

Rigetti differentiates itself through vertical integration.

Unlike many competitors focused purely on research, Rigetti controls multiple layers of the stack:

- Quantum hardware
- Quantum software (Quilt)
- Quantum Cloud Services (QCS)
- Chip fabrication
- Cloud deployment

Its superconducting architecture also enables significantly faster gate speeds than several competing hardware approaches.

More importantly, Rigetti has already established commercial credibility through partnerships with:

- Amazon Braket
- Microsoft Azure Quantum
- DARPA
- AFRL
- Nasdaq
- Deloitte

These relationships generate revenue today while simultaneously validating the technology.

# The Competitive Landscape

Quantum hardware remains fragmented.

Today's leading approaches include:

| Architecture    | Major Companies      | Strengths                                   |
| --------------- | -------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Superconducting | Rigetti, IBM, Google | Fast gate speeds, manufacturing scalability |
| Trapped-Ion     | IonQ, Quantinuum     | High fidelity, long coherence times         |
| Photonic        | PsiQuantum           | Room temperature operation                  |
| Neutral Atom    | QuEra                | High scalability potential                  |

No consensus winner has emerged.

That creates opportunity—but also significant risk.

# Risks

The investment case remains highly speculative.

The biggest risks include:

## Commercial Adoption

Most quantum use cases remain proofs of concept.

Enterprise demand has yet to materialize at scale.

---

## Revenue Concentration

Government contracts still represent the majority of revenues.

Commercial recurring revenue remains limited.

---

## Cash Burn

Rigetti continues investing aggressively in R&D.

Although cash reserves are substantial, profitability remains years away.

---

## Technology Risk

Superconducting qubits may not ultimately become the dominant architecture.

Alternative approaches—including trapped ions, photonic systems and topological qubits—could outperform over time.

The winner of today's quantum race has not yet been decided.

# Final Thoughts

Rigetti isn't a value investment.

It's a technology platform investment.

Buying Rigetti today is less about current revenue and more about assigning probability to a future where quantum computing becomes commercially indispensable.

If quantum hardware reaches practical utility, today's valuation could eventually appear conservative.

If commercialization takes significantly longer—or another hardware architecture emerges as the winner—the downside remains substantial.

For investors comfortable with frontier technology risk, however, Rigetti represents one of the most direct public-market ways to participate in the evolution of quantum computing.

The investment isn't about predicting next quarter's earnings.

It's about deciding whether quantum computing becomes one of the defining technologies of the next decade.
