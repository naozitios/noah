## TTWO should deliver 4x returns in one year

I expect **Take-Two Interactive (TTWO)** to be worth roughly **$1000** in December 2026 **(roughy ~300% growth)**, on a 6.5B EBITDA and a 30 EV/EBITDA multiple (pre GTA6 announcement release multiple) driven by:

1. A step-change in earnings power from **GTA VI**
2. Structurally strong and growing **net bookings**
3. Operational **Scale** and Cross-Game **Synergies**

This is not a call on perfect execution — it’s a view that the **market is underestimating both the durability and magnitude of Take-Two’s earnings expansion** once GTA VI enters the revenue base.

## 1. GTA VI is going to be a major revenue driver for TTWO

The core driver of this thesis is **GTA VI**, but not in the simplistic “big launch = big stock pop” sense. 

My **napkin number** for the sales of GTA within the first two weeks is **$8 billion** *(1.5x the yearly TTWO revenues… i know thats crazy but hear me out)*

I take a forecasted sales count based off the RDR2 2019 first 2 weeks ***sales to average youtube views.*** With an average of 30 million views across the three trailer videos and 23 million sale in the first two weeks of launch, reusing the same model we will expect 115 copies to be sold based on the first GTA trailer views of 270M and 155M.

## 2. Game sales aren’t a once off revenue boost

Strong net bookings give us some confidence on the smooth cyclicality of major releases. Not only does it tell us that GTA 6 could generate more revenue than just its sales, but that its other products are doing well.

This tells us that there is **ongoing player engagement** and not just launch spikes. Recurring consumer spend is quite common in TTWO. 

This matters because bookings:

- Smooth the cyclicality of major releases
- Reflect **ongoing player engagement**, not just launch spikes
- Create operating leverage once fixed development costs are absorbed

In other words, Take-Two is not a “one-hit launch company” — it is already operating with a **hybrid blockbuster + live monetization model**.

## 3. Strong fundamentals

Take-Two is sitting on a ton of data, with the ability to analyse user behaviour from Zynga games to understand player preferences and inform new development. Its value is further amplified by **network effects** and **shared technology platforms** that benefit multiple titles within the portfolio.

For example, the **RAGE engine (Rockstar Advanced Game Engine)** is used to develop both GTA and RDR2, allowing advanced open-world games to share development costs and achieve scale efficiencies. Additionally, the company’s accumulated **know-how can be leveraged across teams**, enhancing productivity and innovation.

### Why this matters

- **Fixed costs are spread across more games**, improving operating leverage
- **Shared data and insights** allow better targeting of player preferences and development priorities

## 4. What needs to be true for this TTWO to quadruple

### GTA 6 does not materially underperform on engagement

If GTA 6 engagement starts to winnow i will have to adjust my price target. Since the expected sales are a multiple of youtube videos to sales, more data points can give us more insight as to whether we are over or underestimating sales. It is worth noting that DFC Intelligence forecasts 40 million sales in the first year (3 billion in sales). So my take is rather contrarian in nature and could be adjusted as more information comes in

### GTA 6 does not delay release

GTA 6 has had a history of delaying its releases, causing whipsaws of 10% drawdowns when they do. Although delays show sign of a constantly improving product, delays can cause investor rotation into more promising higher yielding assets (like semiconductors, as of date of writing)

### Marketing budget does not exceed 1.5B

The model assumes a $1.5B marketing budget within SG&A for GTA 6. If the actual spend exceeds this, we would expect a smaller EBITDA uplift, as higher costs would reduce profitability
