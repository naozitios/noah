## 1.  Apple services revenue to double in 3 years

### **Why am i so bullish on Apple services?**

Growth in Apple Services will be driven primarily by two key pillars: **payments** and **subscription offerings**. Payments such as Apple Pay should scale with rising transaction volumes, while subscription revenues are expected to expand through increasing ecosystem stickiness.

1. **Payment services**
    
    Apple Pay is positioned to be the default payment method across Apple devices, offering *high convenience* at *a low cost*. With a ~0.15% take rate (% Apple keeps as revenue) and historical transaction volume growth of ~15–20%), sustained adoption should drive steady, compounding payments revenue within Apple Services.
    
2. **Apple subscriptions**  
    
    | Measure | **ARPU/ year** |
    | --- | --- |
    | **Apple Music**  | **~$120/year**  |
    | **iCloud**  | **~$17.5–$168/year**  |
    | **AppleTV+** | **~$168/year**  |
    | **Apple Arcade** | **~$120/year**  |
    | **Apple One Bundle** | **~$288/year** ***(individual plan)* or** **$72/year *(family plan)*** |
    | **2025 Blended Services** | **~$14.70/year** |
    | **2029 Forecasted Target ARPU** | **$70/year** |


    1. **Sticky, premium subscriptions**
        
        Apple’s subscription services are positioned as premium offerings. Apple Music wins on user experience and music quality, while Apple TV differentiates through strong curation and creative freedom. 
        
        **Apple Music:** Apple Music competes on superior audio quality through platform ownership. Although its recommendation algorithm currently lags Spotify’s, increasing data and model maturity should narrow this gap over time.
        
        **Apple TV:** Although Apple TV is currently loss-making and attracts negative press, this is a deliberate loss-leader strategy. Over time, high-quality labels, a creator-first culture, and curated discovery will drive hit density and ecosystem lock-in. While the content library is still thin today, producing ~25 shows annually means Apple TV can build meaningful pull and subscriber momentum within four years.
        
    2. **Incredible distribution strategy via Apple’s installed base**
        
        Every device upgrade exposes users to Apple subscriptions, creating a built-in acquisition channel for Apple’s subscription bundle. This constant touchpoint educates users on the value of Apple Services and drives a steady inflow of new subscribers.
        
    3. **Massive TAM with long runway for ARPU expansion**
        
        Blended Services ARPU should expand from ~$15 today toward ~$90 in 2029 as ***subscriptions become default***, attach rates rise, and users gradually layer multiple services rather than relying on a single bundle.
        

### Why this matters

Apple Services is transitioning from a **supporting revenue stream** into a ***core earnings and valuation driver**.* 

- Unlike hardware, Services revenue is recurring, *high-margin (80%),* and scales with the installed base rather than unit shipments. As payments, subscriptions, and commissions deepen their integration into the ecosystem, Apple’s revenue mix shifts structurally toward **more predictable cash flows, higher ROIC, and greater downside protection**.
- Services growth is not dependent on breakthrough innovation. It is driven by **behavioural lock-in**: higher transaction.
    - This creates a compounding effect where modest increases in ARPU translate into outsized absolute revenue growth at Apple’s scale. If blended Services ARPU expands toward ~$70, Services alone becomes large enough to meaningfully re-rate Apple’s multiple.

### What the market is not pricing in

**1. ARPU expansion, not just subscriber growth**

*The market largely frames Apple Services growth as a **subscriber count story**, anchoring expectations to today’s ~$15 blended ARPU.* What’s under appreciated is the **depth of monetisation per user**. Apple does not need every user on Apple One for ARPU to inflect meaningfully—incremental adoption of storage tiers, music, payments, and bundled upgrades is sufficient to drive step-change economics.

**2. Payments as a volume-driven compounding engine**

*Apple Pay is often dismissed due to its **low take rate (~0.15%).*** This misses the point: **payments revenue compounds on transaction volume**, not pricing power. As Apple Pay becomes the default checkout layer across devices, apps, and web, revenue growth is driven by frequency and habit, not margin expansion. At scale, even small take rates on trillions in volume become material—and resilient.

**3. Apple TV+ as a strategic loss leader, not a failed product**

*Negative press around Apple TV+ **profitability obscures its strategic role.*** Apple is intentionally trading near-term losses for long-term ecosystem pull. The market underestimates how curated hits, creative control, and steady content cadence (~25 shows annually) can transform Apple TV from a thin library into a meaningful engagement driver—feeding retention and cross-subscription adoption rather than standalone profitability.

**4. Distribution as a structural advantage**

*While Apple Services is often benchmarked against Spotify and Netflix,* its key advantage lies in distribution through its installed base. Device upgrades, system prompts, and default defaults create recurring, near-zero-CAC (customer acquistion cost) moments of service discovery, driving attach-rate expansion over time. I therefore expect ARPU to approach $70, assuming most users adopt at least one core service by 2029.

## 2.  China market adoption is not quite priced in

*I expect market share in China to go from 25% (as of 2025) to 35% in 5 years (US is at 60%) incremental growth (**0.12 billion new iphone sales spread across 5 years**) 20B+ per year*

**Apple’s premium branding is not match for Huawei**

Apple’s moat is rooted in ***brand-led differentiation rather than raw hardware specs.*** Display quality, silicon-to-software integration across multiple products and services, and increasingly AI-enabled features embedded at the chip and system level **create a user experience that is difficult to replicate.** This allows Apple to sustain premium perception even as marginal hardware improvements across the industry converge.

**Telescope cameras are not as necessary as they are trying to tell us**

Consumers are increasingly indifferent to incremental hardware upgrades once performance reaches “good enough.” Camera resolution, processing power, and screen refresh rates now **exceed the needs of most users**—especially as content consumption shifts toward social media, where **colour science, consistency, and ease of use matter more than absolute quality**. This dynamic favors Apple’s focus on experiential polish over spec competition.

**Geopolitical risk is asymmetric**

Despite ongoing trade tensions, it is unlikely China adopts an “EV-style” exclusionary policy toward Apple. Apple remains deeply embedded in China’s consumer market, supply chain, and employment base, making a hard decoupling economically unattractive in the near to medium term.

### Why this matters

Apple’s competitive advantage is increasingly **structural rather than cyclical**. As hardware differentiation compresses across the industry, Apple’s integrated ecosystem, brand trust, and pricing discipline become more valuable—not less. This enables Apple to defend share in mature markets, expand into price-sensitive regions without brand dilution, and monetise its installed base through Services. Together, these forces support more stable revenue, higher lifetime value per user, and improved earnings visibility.

### What the market is not pricing in

**1. “Good enough” hardware strengthens Apple’s moat**

*The market often assumes slowing hardware innovation weakens Apple’s competitive position.* In reality, it **raises the importance of software, ecosystem, and brand**, areas where Apple is strongest.

**2. China risk is overstated relative to Apple’s embeddedness**

*Bear cases tend to extrapolate worst-case geopolitical outcomes.* What’s under appreciated is Apple’s economic and cultural entrenchment in China, which materially lowers the probability of extreme policy actions.

## 3.  What needs to be true for price to get to **480 in December 2028**

For this thesis to play out, Apple must continue executing across a few key fronts:

- **Sustained performance in subscription investments**
    - Apple TV+ continues delivering a balanced slate of **high-quality, culturally relevant content**
    - Apple Music deepens engagement through **improved discovery algorithms** and **differentiated premium content**
- **Hardware differentiation remains intact**
    - Key competitors, particularly Huawei, **do not close the gap in display and screen technology**
- **Cost and pricing discipline**
    - As the iPhone lineup expands to include more budget-oriented models, improved pricing strategies and lower production costs can support margins without diluting brand equity.
